My capstone project is to develop an autonomous line following walking robot. Utilizing Solidworks, ADAMS, 3D printing, and Arduino, myself and two other group members have been able to work on this project from January 2019 until now - must be completed by December 2019. My contributions to the project have been the design and calculations of the leg mechanisms, while assisting with the designs of the base and side walls. In addition, I have given design advice and recommendations to my group members for how the assembly willfasten together and common practices when using CAD.
This project consisted of myself and a group of three in designing, 3-D printing, and analyzing a hand crank spoiler. I was responsible for the designs of the front cover, assembly casing, and the hand crank. Using NX 10, I coordinated the overall shape and size, as well as the mechanics of the assembly. Additionally, I assisted with the development of the rack and pinion by calculating thesize and teeth, while also using NX to do a stress analysis on the rack when it is in operation.
This project consisted of myself and four others in modelling a Mazda RX-7 13b Wankel engine in Catia V5R19. One group mate and I worked together to dissassemble the engine and clean all the components. We distributed parts to each group member for modelling and I focused on the rotor. I learned a lot on the necessary steps for modelling complex components as the rotor consisted of many small grooves, seals, and gears. In addition, I worked with team mates that modelled the components that connected to the rotor. Once the modelling was finished, our group was able to complete the kinematic motion simulation.
